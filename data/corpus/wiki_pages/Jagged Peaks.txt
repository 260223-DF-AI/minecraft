The jagged peaks are one of the three tall mountain peak biomes. These tall and jagged mountains are completely made out of stone covered by a layer of snow blocks.


== Generation ==
Jagged peaks generate in temperate, cold, and snowy biome temperature ranges, in negative weirdness. This biome often generates next to plains, sunflower plains, flower forests, forests, birch forests, dark forests, taigas, old growth taigas, snowy plains, ice spikes, and snowy taigas. Jagged peaks often border other mountain biomes such as groves, snowy slopes, and rarely stony peaks, and they generate close to meadows, cherry groves, and frozen peaks. Due to the high continentalness and low erosion values, jagged peaks are almost guaranteed to have deep dark generate below them, often bordered by dripstone caves.


== Description ==
Jagged peaks are more likely to generate in taller and more jagged and pointy peaks that often pass the clouds and can peak at Y=256, where they can rarely form large, flat plateaus. It is covered by a single layer of snow blocks with stone underneath often exposing ores such as coal, iron and emerald, and just like the snowy slopes, stone cliffs can generate in steep sides to the east or north with only a layer of snow covering them. Pillager outposts are the only structures that can generate here, and they often form large foundations of cobblestone due to the tall cliffs. Goats can spawn frequently in this biome, and in Bedrock Edition, rabbits and polar bears can also spawn here. 
With Vibrant Visuals, jagged peaks use default environmental settings with a coolish color grading, making the biome appear colder blue. They use cold sunlight, which is slightly brighter at sunrise and sunset.
