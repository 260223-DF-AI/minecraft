Copper boots are a variant of boots crafted from copper ingots. They are the sixth-highest tier of boots, having the same armor points as leather and golden boots but with higher durability.


== Obtaining ==


=== Crafting ===


=== Mob loot ===
Zombies, husks, skeletons, strays, bogged, and parched have a small chance to spawn wearing armor, which may include copper boots. There is a 8.5% chance (9.5% with Looting I, 10.5% with Looting II and 11.5% with Looting III) for the mob to drop the boots they're wearing upon death. The dropped boots are usually badly damaged, and may be enchanted with enchantment levels 5-19.


== Usage ==
When worn, copper boots provide 1 () armor points.


=== Repairing ===
Two damaged pairs of copper boots can be combined through crafting or using the grindstone. The durability of the used copper boots are added together, plus an extra 5% durability. Both the crafting table and grindstone methods do not keep the enchantments of either boots.


==== Combining ====


==== Unit repair ====

Copper boots can be repaired in an anvil by adding units of the tiers' repair material, with each repair material restoring 25% the boots' maximum durability, rounded down. Two copper boots of the same tier can also be combined in an anvil. Both methods preserve a copper boots' enchantments.

=== Armor trimming ===
It is possible to upgrade armor with trims. This requires a smithing template, a boots, and an ingot or crystal (iron ingot, copper ingot, gold ingot, netherite ingot, emerald, redstone dust, lapis lazuli, amethyst shard, nether quartz, diamond, or resin brick).
These trims have no effect on the gameplay or strength of the armor.
