This page lists texture colors that do not fit into any other category. Only colors that are applied to textures are listed; other colors such as sky and fog are not listed here.


== List ==


=== Rendering ===
Blocks and items in the Creative inventory have a darkened tint attached to them on Java Edition. If all textures are set to  #FFFFFF, then all items, single sided blocks, and the top faces of all other blocks, are rendered slightly darker at about 98.8% brightness  #FCFCFC, the bottom left side of blocks at about 64.3% brightness  #A4A4A4, and the bottom right side at 39.6% brightness  #656565. In full brightness, blocks render at 98.8% brightness  #FCFCFC on the top, 79.2% brightness  #CACACA on the Z-axis, and 59.2% brightness  #979797 on the X-axis.
This is due a rendering bug: MC-213446 and MC-123703.

=== Entities ===


==== Experience orbs ====
Experience orb textures are mostly white, gray, and red; a gradient is applied afterward to make them green and yellow.

=== Light colors ===
When the "Render Dragon Features for Creators" experiment is enabled in Minecraft Preview, and Vibrant Visuals is enabled, all light-emitting blocks render colored lighting on the surrounding blocks. Furthermore, if point lights are enabled, some light blocks have point lighting, which is a directional light source with the same colors applied. The strength depends on the block's light level. Blocks not listed here use the default light colors.[more information needed]


=== Wolf and cat collars ===
Both wolf and cat collars use the dye colors directly one to one.


=== Other world elements ===


==== Banners ====
Banners are colored according to default dye colors.


==== Beacon Beam ====
Beacon beams are colored according to default dye colors. Without any stained glass, it defaults to the color for white dye.


==== End Gateway beam ====


==== Guardian beam ====

Guardian beams are colored based on the progress of the attack and linearly transition from  #402080 to  #FFDF40. When the attack is past 80 ticks it causes the red color to overflow, which colors the beam  #00E040 as intended.


==== World border ====


=== Dyed signs ===
The text on dyed signs uses the following colors.[verify for Bedrock Edition] On signs with non-glowing ink, the colors are darkened by 60% from the values in the table. On signs with glowing ink, the colors in the table are used directly for the text, and the outline has the same darkened color as text on signs with regular ink, except for black text, which has an outline of  #F0EBCC. Due to MC-123703  resolved as "Works as intended"., the actual rendered colors are slightly darker.
