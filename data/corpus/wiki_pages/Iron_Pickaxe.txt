An iron pickaxe is a variant of pickaxe that is crafted from iron ingots. It is the third-highest tier of pickaxe, having higher mining efficiency, durability, attack damage, and enchantability than a copper pickaxe. It has a mining level of 2, allowing it to mine gold ore, redstone ore, emerald ore, and diamond ore.

=== Mob loot ===
In Bedrock Edition, pillagers and vindicators have a chance of dropping a damaged iron pickaxe when killed during a raid. The pickaxe has a 50% chance of being enchanted with random enchantment(s).
In Bedrock Edition, wandering traders have a chance of 8.5% (increased by 1% per level of Looting) to drop an enchanted iron pickaxe (available enchantments listed below) when killed while showing the item as trade offer, if the player holds an emerald.


=== Trading ===
Both toolsmiths and wandering traders can sell an enchanted iron pickaxe.


== Usage ==


=== Mining ===
An iron pickaxe is used to break stone and metal-based materials faster. Breaking a block with an iron pickaxe consumes one use (one durability point). No durability is consumed for blocks that have a hardness value of 0, such as torches and flowers.


==== Mining level ====
Iron pickaxes have a mining level of 2. An iron pickaxe or better is required to mine the following blocks:

Block of Diamond
Block of Emerald
Block of Gold
Block of Raw Gold
Deepslate Diamond Ore
Deepslate Emerald Ore
Deepslate Gold Ore
Deepslate Redstone Ore
Diamond Ore
Emerald Ore
Gold Ore
Redstone Ore


=== Weapon ===


==== Java Edition ====
All pickaxes have an attack speed modifier of -2.8, meaning they take about 0.85 seconds to recover. Iron pickaxes do 4HP damage per attack, or 4.8 damage per second.


==== Bedrock Edition ====
All pickaxes attack instantly, with iron pickaxes doing 5HP damage per attack.


=== Repairing ===


==== Combining ====


==== Anvil repair ====

An iron pickaxe can be repaired in an anvil by adding iron ingots, with each ingot restoring 25% of the pickaxe's maximum durability, rounded down. Two iron pickaxes can also be combined in an anvil. Both methods preserve the pickaxe's enchantments.
