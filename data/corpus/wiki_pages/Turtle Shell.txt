A turtle shell is a variant of helmet that is crafted from turtle scutes. It is the third-highest tier of helmet, having the same armor points as golden, copper, iron, and chainmail helmets but with higher durability. When equipped, it grants ten seconds of Water Breathing to a player who enters water. It is also a brewing ingredient for potions of the Turtle Master.

== Usage ==


=== Turtle shell effect ===
While equipped by a player, the turtle shell provides the Water Breathing status effect, allowing the wearer to remain underwater for an additional 10 seconds. The effect immediately recharges after exposure to air. It does not recharge if the player enters a bubble column. The Water Breathing effect does not activate for other mobs.

==== Combining ====


==== Unit repair ====

A turtle shell can be repaired in an anvil by adding turtle scutes, with each scute restoring 25% the turtle shell's maximum durability, rounded down. Two turtle shells can also be combined in an anvil. Both methods preserve the helmet's enchantments.


=== Enchantments ===


=== Armor trimming ===
It is possible to upgrade turtle shells with trims. This requires a smithing template, a turtle shell, and an ingot or crystal (iron ingot, copper ingot, gold ingot, netherite ingot, emerald, redstone dust, lapis lazuli, amethyst shard, nether quartz, diamond, or resin brick).
These trims have no effect on the gameplay or strength of the armor.

== Trivia ==
The turtle shell is the only helmet that is not a part of a complete set.
Defense point values exist in the code for all other pieces of a theoretical "turtle" armor set. These are: 6 () for chestplates, 5 () for leggings, and 2 () for boots, which is identical to a full set of iron.
