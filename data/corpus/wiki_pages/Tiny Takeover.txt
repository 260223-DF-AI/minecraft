Tiny Takeover is a game drop that was released on March 24, 2026 as Java Edition 26.1 and Bedrock Edition 26.10. Its theme is "cuteness" and "emotional attachment with mobs". It visually overhauls baby mobs and rabbits, makes name tags craftable, and adds the golden dandelion, which when fed to baby mobs, stops them from aging.
During development, the update was referred to as Drop 1 of 2026. The final name of the update was revealed on Minecraft.net and Minecraft Monthly on March 3, 2026.
Some features of this drop were released as part of an experiment in Bedrock Edition 26.0.


== Additions ==


=== Blocks ===
 Golden dandelion

A new golden variant of the regular dandelion.
Does not generate naturally.
Can be crafted using a single dandelion surrounded with eight gold nuggets.
Prevents baby mobs from aging when fed, showing downward green particles.
When fed again, the mob will continue to age again, showing upward green particles.
Cannot be used on baby undead mobs, piglins, and villagers.
Can be used to make suspicious stew of the Saturation effect.
Cannot be composted or used to attract bees.
Attracts piglins.


=== Sounds ===
Note block

Added the trumpet instrument.
It can be heard when a note block is placed on top of any of the following copper blocks:
Block of Copper
Cut Copper and its variants.
Chiseled Copper
The sound is different based on the oxidation level of the copper blocks.
Mobs

Added sound variants for the following animals:
Cat: Royal
Chicken: Picky
Cow: Moody
Pig: Mini and Big
The already existing mob sounds are now part of the Classic sound variant.
Added eating sounds for pigs.


== Changes ==


=== Items ===
 Armor

Helmets, chestplates, leggings, and boots now have unique textures and models when worn by the baby variants of zombies, husks, drowned, zombie villagers, piglins, and zombified piglins.
Does not display trims in that form.
Name tag

Can now be crafted using a single nugget of any kind and a paper.

No longer sold by librarians.
No longer found in chests in woodland mansions and ancient cities.
Can now be sold by wandering traders as a common trade for 1 emerald with a 7% chance, and 5 trades in stock.


=== Mobs ===
Updated the models and textures of the following baby mobs:

Carpets, chests, horse armor, saddles, and wolf armor no longer render on baby variants of the mobs stated above, when applicable.
Changed sounds for kittens, chicks, foals, baby pigs, and puppies. Now uses its own sounds instead of high pitched adult sounds.
 Horse

The black-dot pattern of the adult horse has been changed to better match with the new foal.
Librarian

No longer offers name tags as a trade.
Now offers red candles and yellow candles for the price of three emeralds (per candle) at the Master level.
 Rabbit

The models, animations, and textures have been updated.
 Now has an idle animation.


=== Gameplay ===
Stonecutter

Can now directly turn stone and deepslate into their respective cobbled variants.


== Promotions ==
Throughout the development of Tiny Takeover, it was described as the "cutest" game drop.


=== Downloadable content ===

On March 24, a skin pack entitled Tiny Takeover: Mob Caretakers was released, consisting of three characters themed after the mobs added in the update. An add-on, Tiny Takeover: Mini Mania, was announced the next day.


=== Social Media Takeover ===
Beginning with the announcement of the title in Minecraft Monthly, the update was marketed with the gimmick of baby or otherwise small mobs "taking over" the Minecraft franchise. Within the announcement, there is a running gag of Tiny Agnes and Tiny Vu trying to hack the show and host it instead. On the same day, Minecraft.net was updated with several baby mobs infesting design elements, peeking out of a link to Spring to Life - Official Minecraft Trailer, hovering the Mounts of Mayhem site header, and appearing on the icons for various social media platforms. In the article about the name, Linn Viberg's Mojavatar became covered in chicks, and several previously released promotional images were altered to show chicks interacting with the borders. 
This extended even after Minecraft LIVE – March 2026 was announced, with both a trailer and promotional video featuring a takeover of chicks. Within the show itself, the sets were even decorated to be overflowing with baby mobs, giving the appearance of a taken over space. Another trailer was also released, once again centering around babies invading Minecraft. Once the update released, changelogs used images of this same scenario; one in a field full of them, the others seemingly chasing Efe.


=== Reward Drops ===

Three special reward drops are available to promote the release of the game drop. One is available through TikTok, and the other two are available through Twitch.


=== Community Challenges ===

Two community challenges were announced on March 25, 2026, to promote the release of the game drop. The first challenge began on March 27 and ended on March 30. The second challenge began on April 3 and ended on April 6. Players in Bedrock Edition get rewarded Character Creator items and players in Java Edition get rewarded a skin pack that will also be released to reward progress.


==== Tiny Takeover Community Giveaway ====

A special promotion on Gleam.io where fans submit Minecraft fan art. Players in Bedrock Edition get rewarded with a Character Creator item and players in Java Edition get rewarded with a skin pack.


=== Tiny Takeover: Mini Mania ===

Tiny Takeover: Mini Mania is an official free add-on available on the Marketplace, described as an "add-on where you get to prove your skills in adorable challenges for equally adorable rewards".
