Copper armor is a type of armor made from copper ingots. It is the sixth strongest armor, between gold and leather.

=== Mob loot ===
In Normal and Hard difficulties, zombies, husks, skeletons, strays, and bogged have a chance to spawn with armor, with a 32.23% chance of it being copper armor. There is a 8.5% chance (9.5% with Looting I, 10.5% with Looting II and 11.5% with Looting III) for the mob to drop the armor they're wearing upon death. The dropped armor is usually badly damaged, and may be enchanted with enchantment levels 5-19.


== Usage ==

Wearing copper armor reduces damage taken from most damage types.


=== Statistics ===
When a full set of copper armor is worn, the set provides 10 () armor points.


=== Damage reduction ===

Wearing armor reduces incoming damage according to two values: armor, and armor toughness. Each piece of armor provides a given armor and armor toughness value, and the values from each armor piece are added together when calculating damage reduction.
The strength of incoming damage also determines the damage reduction of worn armor, as armor does not protect in a linear fashion.

=== Equipping ===
Players can equip copper armor by placing it in their inventory, using the item, or via a dispenser. When equipped, the armor is rendered. Some other humanoids can also render the armor, pick it up, equip it, and be equipped via a dispenser; see the table below.

In Java Edition, with the exception of the allay, dolphin, fox, and panda, all other mobs with the CanPickUpLoot nbt tag true (1b) can equip armor from a dispenser (except happy ghast), and pick up and equip if are without armor or have worse armor (except ender dragon). All living entities can equip armor using commands, like /item or /data.
