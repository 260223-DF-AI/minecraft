The Nether wastes or hell[Bedrock Edition  only] is the most common biome found in the Nether.


== Description ==
The Nether wastes is the most common Nether biome and makes up around 36% of the Nether by volume. Unlike other Nether biomes, which tend to generate in large patches, the Nether wastes more often generates in thinner strips, often separating two or more other biomes. In particular, a thin strip of Nether wastes always separates the crimson forest and the basalt deltas, or the warped forest and soul sand valley, pairs of biomes that can never border each other.
The Nether wastes is a barren biome composed of mostly netherrack. It has extensive lava seas with glowstone blobs hanging from the ceiling. Blobs of Nether quartz, Nether gold ore, and blackstone generate in the wastes (where ore is most visible) with the rarer ancient debris found unexposed within the terrain. Gravel and soul sand are found among its coastlines.
Most of the Nether's mobs can spawn here, including ghasts, zombified piglins, magma cubes, striders, piglins, and the occasional endermen. Nether fortresses, ruined portals and bastion remnants are present as well.

== Trivia ==
In Bedrock Edition, if the Nether wastes is used as the biome for Overworld generation, although the terrain is entirely netherrack, no ore is generated (including Nether quartz ore, Nether gold ore, ancient debris, etc.). Caverns and lakes (with red-colored water) still generate. The player is still able to place water in an Overworld Nether wastes biome, but trying to sleep in a bed still causes an explosion.
