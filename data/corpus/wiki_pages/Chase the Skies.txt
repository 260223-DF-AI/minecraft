Chase the Skies is a game drop that released on June 17, 2025, as Java Edition 1.21.6 and Bedrock Edition 1.21.90. It introduces happy ghasts which can be ridden with harnesses, improved mechanics for leads, craftable saddles, new background music, the "Tears" music disc, the player locator bar, as well as Vibrant Visuals in Bedrock Edition. The first features of this game drop were revealed at Minecraft LIVE – March 2025.
During development, the update was referred to as Summer Drop 2025 and Drop 2 2025 as the name of the Bedrock Edition experiment. The final name of the update was revealed in a minecraft.net article on June 1, 2025.
Experimental features for the update were released prior to the finalized release as part of Bedrock Edition 1.21.80.


== Additions ==


=== Blocks ===
 Dried ghast

Naturally generates next to Nether fossils in soul sand valleys.
Can be obtained through bartering with piglins.
Crafted with soul sand and 8 ghast tears.
When put in water, the block's appearance changes 3 times depending on the time spent hydrated, changing its facial expression. After this, it becomes a ghastling.

=== Mobs ===
 Happy ghast

A passive version of the ghast, which can be equipped with a harness and ridden by up to four players.
Can be stood on, similar to a shulker.
When not being ridden, will approach players holding snowballs.
 Baby version is called a ghastling.
Have a unique texture and sounds.
Spawned after a dried ghast gets hydrated in water.
Follows any passive or neutral animal.
Can be fed snowballs to speed up their growth.


=== Sounds ===

Now emits ambient sounds when above 2 sand, red sand, terracotta or stained terracotta blocks.


=== GUI ===
Locator bar

The locator bar is a new UI element on the player HUD that displays the direction of other players in the world.
When the locatorBar game rule is enabled and there are multiple players in a world, the locator bar will appear on-screen for each player, replacing the experience bar. The player's experience level will still be visible as before above the locator bar.
The locator bar will display colored indicators when the camera is facing within 120 degrees towards another player. If another player is 30 degrees above or below the camera's viewport (the screen), an up or down arrow will be displayed.
Players in Spectator mode are not visible on the locator bar.
A player can hide and prevent their indicator from appearing on the locator bar via the following means:
Sneaking.
Wearing a head or a carved pumpkin.
Being under the Invisibility effect.
Can be disabled by map creator or server operator.
The locator bar can be used to locate entities other than the player, controlled by waypoint_transmit_range and waypoint_receive_range attributes.
The locator bar waypoint styles can be changed by /waypoint command.‌[JE  only]
The locator bar is in place of the EXP bar, but the EXP bar will show for a short time after collecting EXP.

== Changes ==


=== Blocks ===
Sand and Red sand

No longer emits windy sounds.
Terracotta and Stained terracotta

No longer emit ambient sounds.


=== Items ===
Lead

Is now crafted with 5 string, with the former slimeball in its recipe replaced by an additional piece of string.

Entities can now be attached to other entities with leads.
Can now be removed from entities using shears, including dispensers containing shears.
Snapping distance has been increased to 12 blocks.
Leather

Can now be found in monster room, ancient city, desert pyramid, jungle pyramid and stronghold altar chests in quantities 1-5, replacing saddles.
Saddle

Can now be crafted with 3 leather and 1 iron ingot.

Can now be removed from mobs wearing them by using shears on the mob.
Cannot be removed this way from ravagers or mobs with riders.
Can no longer be equipped using dispensers.
No longer found in chests in monster rooms, ancient cities, desert pyramids, jungle pyramids and in stronghold altar chests.
Shears

Can now remove saddles, horse armor, harnesses and carpets from mobs.
Can now cut leads.
They cannot be removed if the mob has a rider.
They cannot be removed if the player is crouching.
Saddles cannot be removed from ravagers.
Dispensers with shears cannot remove saddles, horse armor, harnesses and carpets equipped on mobs.
Carrot

Can now be used to tame, heal, and grow horses, donkeys, and mules.
 Spawn eggs

Change blaze and breeze spawn egg sizes to more accurately represent the mobs.
Splash potions

Now have a duration that varies based on distance from the impact point.
If a splash potion directly hits an entity, the entity receives the full duration and potency of the effect.
However, if the entity is further away from the impact, the effect's duration and potency decrease linearly.


=== Mobs ===
 Ghast

The resolution of the ghast texture has been doubled to match the new happy ghasts.
Now drops a ”Tears” music disc when player kills it using a deflected fireball.


=== Graphics ===
Clouds

The texture for cloud distribution has been changed.
Now has more, smaller gaps between the clouds.
Clouds are of a more consistent, oval shape than before.
Now has a creeper face made up of clouds in a certain section of the texture.
Cloud render distance has been increased.

== Promotions ==


=== Giveaway ===

To promote the release of the drop, a contest was held to give away ten travel-themed prizes. These included a hammock, a blanket, a weekender bag, Crocs, a thermos, and a creeper-themed cooler bag. To participate, players were tasked with taking a screenshot of gameplay, possibly using Vibrant Memories. Up to five photos per day were eligible.


=== Vibrant Memories ===

The day following the drop's release, an add-on was published to promote the introduction of Vibrant Visuals. Developed by Oreville Studios, the add-on allows players to immortalize specific locations and memories in their worlds.


=== Take on Ghastastic Challenges ===
 

To promote the release of the drop, an advertising campaign began on June 20, 2025, featuring two community challenges. For Bedrock players, they earned the two items. For Java players, a skin pack was also released to reward progress.
