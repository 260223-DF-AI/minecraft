Ten Things You Probably Didn't Know About Minecraft is an educational comedy series on the Minecraft YouTube channel. The series is centered around M.A.R.I.L.L.A., a sentient computer left behind in a Mojang Studios closet, attempting to teach the viewer various pieces of trivia.


== Series 1 ==


=== Village & Pillage ===


==== Summary ====
The video begins inside of a broom closet for the first time, as M.A.R.I.L.L.A. boots up in the wake of a door closing. Introducing herself as "Mojang's favorite piece of obsolete hardware," she establishes that she's long out of use, and segues into a list of ten trivia facts on Village & Pillage. The tenth fact is the introduction of pandas to the game, highlighting an issue wherein pandas startled by sneezing would become stuck in midair. This culminates in a gag of the cover for Xbox 360 Edition being changed to include a warning prohibiting players from touching the game if they have a common cold. A panda then sneezes again, corrupting the footage.
The ninth fact is about cats, joking about how they tend to spawn inside of libraries and are perceived to flee. This culminates in a joke list of books to add to the game, so they can "appeal" to the cats. The eight fact is about another glitch, highlighting an issue where baby villagers would lose their heads thanks to complications with the profession overlays. Similarly, the seventh fact is about the "fox box," a corruption of the fox model, which M.A.R.I.L.L.A. calls nightmare fuel and proceeds to make puns about. From there M.A.R.I.L.L.A. explains that the introduction of "attics" to the forge building allowed for monsters to spawn, expressing gratitude for there not being any hostile mobs in the real world.
The fifth fact touches on villagers utilizing beds, joking that they are suffering from insomnia due to sporadic head movements, and at one point another decapitation glitch. M.A.R.I.L.L.A. then takes a pot shot at phantoms, before sneezing herself. The fourth fact touches on the worldbuilding exercised by the village redesigns, where they're made entirely out of local materials such as sandstone and the colors of beds. The third fact is about pillagers, highlighting their appearance in Minecraft Dungeons. She refers to them in unkind terms, and credits their conception to Jens Bergensten. The penultimate fact touches on the sound design of the horn used at the start of raids. She said it was designed to be easy to understand but also to feel "odd", in order to match the "unique characteristics" of the pillagers. The final fact touches on villagers without an associated village, particularly those that can spawn in swamps, which M.A.R.I.L.L.A. finds to be cute.

=== The Last Ten Years ===


==== Summary ====
M.A.R.I.L.L.A., having apparently been trapped inside of a closet for ten years, decides to list off ten pieces of trivia for the 10th anniversary of Minecraft. The first piece of trivia is that the game "almost never existed!", meaning it had several precursors, such as RubyDung. M.A.R.I.L.L.A. finds this name preferable, and creates a list of other names that she feels would have been more profitable. The second piece discusses the introduction of crafting to the game in 2010, and then discusses MATTIS, the crafting system in Pocket Edition Alpha. While she explains the acronym, a contradictory one is spelled out on-screen, "Maybe Another Top Ten is Stupid." She then talks about how MATTIS is named after a Mojang employee, who apparently distracted the writer of the video over a stamp collection.
The video cuts to a sponsorship from "Squiggly Farms Home-Spawned Squid Milk," which leads to the fact that squid used to be milkable. The fourth trivia is about witches, who aren't milkable, but are based off the Russian folklore Baba Yaga, which they refuse to show an image of. The next trivia is about horses, which originated from the mod Mo' Creatures. Upon seeing the rest of the mobs in the mod, M.A.R.I.L.L.A. has an episode. The next scene talks about spectator mode, originally just a developer tool. M.A.R.I.L.L.A. uses it to go into Baba Yaga's house.
After showing off various achievements from human history, M.A.R.I.L.L.A. segues into the sixth fact, the 2015 world record of "most people dressed as Steve." After that, M.A.R.I.L.LA. tells a story about a revolution over a refusal to restore squid milk to the game, endangering millions of actual squids, ending with the annihilation of mankind. However, M.A.R.I.L.L.A. says this will happen in 2019, not 2016. The real fact is that Minecraft won the BAFTA Children's Award. In 2017, talks about a limited edition Xbox One S made to look like a dirt block, which she initially confuses with an actual block of dirt, before talking about several easter eggs, such as a hidden message in the Standard Galactic Alphabet. The final segment is about the Update Aquatic, and how bubble columns could jettison squids out of the ocean, and scout locations for the uprising.

== Trivia ==
Throughout the series, M.A.R.I.L.L.A. makes several pop culture references and puns.
In Village & Pillage,  M.A.R.I.L.L.A. lists off several books made to appeal to cats. These are largely puns on existing media, including Nineteen Eighty-Four, The Great Gatsby, To Kill a Mockingbird, The Handmai'd Tail, The Catcher in the Rye, Eat, Pray, Love, Leaves of Grass, Catch-22, and the musical Cats.
In The Last Ten Years, "Avengers Engame" is listed as an alternative name for Minecraft, referencing the 2019 film of the same name. Another name, Fifty Shades of Clay, is a pun on the 2011 erotic fiction novel Fifty Shades of Grey. The name Minecraft: Battle Royale is also a reference to the battle royale genre of video games, which became popular in 2019 due to games such as Fortnite. Later in the video, a picture of the Space Shuttle Columbia is shown, which became infamous for an accident that resulted in the death of eleven astronauts onboard.
During Hostile Mobs, a magazine can be seen labeled "Magnificent Mobs Monthly, 35" a parody of tabloids. On the cover, "Selena Gamez" is quoted saying "Enderman is Mine," referencing actress and pop singer Selena Gomez. Two others are shown, "Hrrrm!" which gives "hottest news from tele Portland," and "Hostile Hotties."
During the final scene of Passive & Neutral Mobs, several comments from fictional viewers are shown. One of them mentions the YouTuber Philza, the viewer asking for a video on him to be made. Another is from the character Saul Goodman, from the television series Breaking Bad.
The map of the world used to show the Squid Uprising is a teal recolor of an asset used during MINECON Earth 2017.
The spaceship shown in Hostile Mobs is a stock asset sold on iStock and similar platforms.
