Closed captions (formerly and commonly referred to as subtitles) display text for sounds in-game. This feature is mainly intended for players who have hearing impairments, are deaf, or play with sounds off. They can also be used for hearing quieter sounds, locating sounds, or identifying sounds a player doesn't recognize. 


== Usage ==
in Java Edition, closed captions can be enabled in the "Accessibility" section of the options menu, as well as in the "Music & Sounds" options. 
In Bedrock Edition, a toggle does not exist and they can only be enabled from options.txt.[not in future version] There are also various settings[upcoming] to customize closed captions, namely the time they are shown, whether to hide a player's own sounds, whether to hide environmental and weather sounds, and the location of the closed captions. If they are enabled using options.txt in the main game, they will not function properly and will not show captions for most sounds.
When closed captions are turned on, a black box appears above the bottom right corner in Java Edition. In Bedrock Edition, they appear on the opposite side of the chat by default (usually top-right or slightly lower depending on other HUD elements), or the bottom-right, and it uses the same style as the chat. Specifically, the background opacity of closed captions is affected by the "Chat Background Opacity" option, and the font color can be adjusted in the chat settings, which also allows to enable Noto Sans.
Here, sounds in the world that are nearby to the player are listed. If the sounds are off-screen, "<" or ">" point in the direction the sound is coming from. As the sound fades away, the text also fades, becoming less white.
All sounds (whether environmental, from entities, block updates, or other sources) have their own individualized closed captions. For entities, the entity is displayed followed by a specific verb, e.g. "Rabbit squeaks" or "Villager mumbles". However, the object is not specified for removing or placing blocks and the sound of footsteps.
