MINECON 2011 was the first official Minecraft convention, held at Mandalay Bay in Las Vegas, Nevada, USA, on November 18, 2011. The event coincided with the official release of Minecraft.
The convention attracted 4,500 attendees, ranging from newborns to 105 years old, with visitors from 24 countries.


== Location ==
The event was held at Mandalay Bay, a 43-story luxury resort and casino in Las Vegas. According to Lydia Winters, the location was chosen because it was where the Mojang team wanted to go at the time.


== Tickets ==
Tickets went on sale on August 11, 2011, 99 days before the convention. Early tickets were priced at $99 until September 30, after which the price increased to $139. Tickets sold out by October 28. The ticket price did not include hotel accommodations, flights, or other expenses. Notch stated on Twitter that MINECON would be a financial loss but an "emotional win" for Mojang.
The first 500 ticket purchasers were entered into a drawing for "The Ultimate Fan Experience", which included a paid hotel room at Mandalay Bay, VIP seating for keynotes and presentations, and a meal with the Mojang team. The winner was a player named Joseph.


== Event highlights ==

MINECON 2011 featured numerous activities, including:

The official release of Minecraft.
Keynote speeches from members of the Minecraft community, including Notch.
Building contests and breakout classes on various Minecraft topics.
Costume contests, exhibits, and opportunities to meet Minecraft personalities.
Commemorative merchandise.
A Kickoff Party open to all attendees.
An "Into the Nether" Party featuring deadmau5, restricted to attendees aged 21 and over.


== Promotions ==


=== Cape ===

Notch announced that MINECON 2011 attendees would receive an exclusive cape. Initially intended to be a secret, the announcement was quickly retracted but later confirmed. On September 22, a vote was opened to decide the design of the cape. Cape #2, which received 42% of the votes, was chosen as the MINECON 2011 cape. Cape #1, which received 33% of the votes, was later used for the MINECON 2012 cape. The cape was distributed to attendees on October 1011.
