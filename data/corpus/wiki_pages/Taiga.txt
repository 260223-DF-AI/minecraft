The taiga is a humid and cold biome comprising a grassland forested with small and large ferns, pale aquamarine-green grass, spruce and pine trees. Foxes commonly spawn here.


== Generation ==
Taigas are frequently bordered by old growth taigas, forests, birch forests, snowy taigas, groves and swamps. They may also generate in the same temperature range as snowy plains and snowy taiga, with frozen rivers running through them; these taigas are almost guaranteed to have lush caves underground. Taigas that generate in temperatures of -0.150 to -0.450 frequently generate lush caves. In addition, when taigas generate far inland, dripstone caves may generate underneath them instead.


== Description ==
The taiga's surface is a grassland with a large number of ferns and large ferns, and is forested with pine and spruce trees, which are the only trees that can generate in this biome. The vegetation also includes short grass, dandelions, poppies and sweet berry bushes, and takes an aquamarine color tone. The water coloration has a unique dark turquoise tone in Bedrock Edition.
With Vibrant Visuals, taigas use a coolish humid volumetric fog setting, which slightly fades the distance in an orange tint. Taigas have coolish color grading tints the environment with a colder blue color, and cold sunlight which appears slightly brighter at dusk and dawn.
Rainfall is common and changes to snowfall above the layer Y=160±8 within taigas due to the humid and cold climate. 
Pillager outposts, villages, and trail ruins generate in taigas occasionally. These villages are made of spruce wood materials and often contain gardens with poppy and pumpkin farms.
