Copper leggings are a variant of leggings crafted from copper ingots. They are the sixth-highest tier of leggings, having the same armor points as golden leggings but with higher durability.


== Obtaining ==


=== Crafting ===


=== Mob loot ===
Zombies, husks, skeletons, strays, and bogged have a small chance to spawn wearing armor, which may include copper leggings. There is a 8.5% chance (9.5% with Looting I, 10.5% with Looting II and 11.5% with Looting III) for the mob to drop the leggings they're wearing upon death. The dropped leggings are usually badly damaged, and may be enchanted with enchantment levels 5-19.


== Usage ==
When worn, copper leggings provide 3 () armor points.


=== Repairing ===


==== Combining ====
Two damaged pairs of copper leggings can be combined through crafting, using the grindstone or the anvil. The durability of the used copper leggings is added together, plus an extra 5% durability.
Crafting and using the grindstone do not keep the enchantments of either copper leggings.

Using the anvil preserves enchantments, but costs experience.


==== Unit repair ====
Copper leggings can also be repaired in an anvil by adding copper ingots, with each ingot restoring 25% the copper leggings' maximum durability, rounded down. This preserves the leggings' enchantments, but costs experience.

=== Armor trimming ===
It is possible to upgrade armor with trims. This requires a smithing template, a copper leggings, and an ingot or crystal (iron ingot, copper ingot, gold ingot, netherite ingot, emerald, redstone dust, lapis lazuli, amethyst shard, nether quartz, diamond, or resin brick).
These trims have no effect on the gameplay or strength of the armor.
