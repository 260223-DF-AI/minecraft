Pac-Man is a  Marketplace map developed by Gamemode One that crosses over with the popular Pac-Man video game franchise, featuring many characters from across its history.


== Arcade Machine ==
The Arcade Machine is the primary focus of the world, being an adaptation of a traditional Pac-Man game. In order to start a game, the player will need to interact with a literal arcade machine, cycling through the selection of mazes by hitting and using to select it.

=== Items & Blocks ===
 PAC-AXE
Allows the player to break blocks, including walls and Pacores.
Pacore
Found inside maze walls, and when mined it grants the player 1000 points. It takes some time to mine.
Key
Found when breaking walls, and they are used to open doors in the hedge maze in Pac-Man World.
Bonus Fruit
They come in a variety and double the points given. And there's a variety of them:
Cherry, Apple, Mango, Banana, Apple, Watermelon, Orange, Grape, Strawberry & Pear: Multiply the points given by eating pellets and heals stamina.
Warp Fruit: Teleports you to a random area.
Golden Apple: Gives you an extra life.
Slop: Grants a random effect.

=== Maze Editor ===
This is an area where you can build your own maze in a 21x19 grid. And any open ends on the left and right become gateways.
You can rest your maze, or test the maze to see if it's possible to beat.


=== Pac-Man Picker ===
As opposed to having conventional skins usable outside of the map, the player must unlock several skins by exploring the map. In turn, the player can only use those skins hardcoded into the map, and can select using the Pac-Man Picker.


=== Pac-Man World ===
Pac-Man World is a city with a hedge maze blocking access to the skins, and Pacore. This is also where Pac-Man Offices (Break Room) is located.
The hedge maze and the city also has pellets that you can eat for extra points.

== Trivia ==
Due to ongoing legal troubles concerning the character, Ms. Pac-Man was barred from appearing in the map.
Years following the release of the map, Minecraft and Pac-Man would indirectly collaborate again, appearing as DLC in Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds.
