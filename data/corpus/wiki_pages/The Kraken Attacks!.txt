The Kraken Attacks! is the seventh episode of the second season of Minecraft Mini-Series.


== Summary ==
As he watches the team on their sand pillar to be eaten by the Kraken, Poseidon affirms that he did not steal the show to get ratings, but to force them to collect all enchanted eyes of ender. As a last request, Durango asks him about the use of the ender eyes and the great prize, while Toby's request is to set his friends free. To answer Durango's question, Poseidon uses the ender eyes to open a portal to Minecraft's Overworld, telling him that he will use his powers to rule over not just the Greek Isles, but also the Overworld.
Seeing this, Theseus feels guilty for helping his father defy Zeus and rushes to stop him to no avail. As a final argument, he steals an enchanted ender eye, breaking the portal, and runs away as his father chases him. On the sand pillar, the Kraken pulls out a slab of sand, and a hopeless CommuniKate laments that if the danger isn't stopped, the team will become fish food. Toby then corrects Kate by telling her that the Kraken is a squid, not a fish. Inspired by the idea, River suggests distracting the Kraken with the fish she and Toby kept in their inventory. As the pillar collapses and all the team is on the water, the Kraken nearly reaches CommuniKate until it gets distracted by the fish thrown by River and Toby, and all the prisoners swim to the beach.
On the beach, Theseus trips on Poseidon's hourglass and drops the ender eye behind him. Quinn quickly retrieves the eye and throws it at Durango, causing Poseidon to accidentally drop his trident. The entire team then plays catch against him by letting him catch the eye only for his prisoners to claim it. When Toby's turn came, he throws the ender eye at Zed, who throws it poorly, and the eye lands in front of Poseidon. To stop him, Durango makes use of the trident, with Theseus trying to stop him to no avail. He threatens Poseidon to turn him into a chicken only for the spell to fail. Toby speculates that the spell only works with a Greek god wielding it, and Theseus reveals that Poseidon is by no means a god, but a mere mortal. Poseidon then reveals that the source of his power is his command block status, which he will use to destroy the Overworld.
