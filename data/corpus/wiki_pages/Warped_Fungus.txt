A warped fungus is a mushroom-like block that generates in the Nether.


== Obtaining ==


=== Breaking ===
A warped fungus can be mined instantly and always drops itself. Mining a flower pot containing a fungus drops both items separately.


=== Natural generation ===
Warped fungi naturally generate in the warped forest and crimson forest biomes, with warped fungus most commonly generating in its respective biome, the warped forest and less frequently in the other, the crimson forest.


=== Mob loot ===
An enderman holding a warped fungus drops the fungus upon death.


=== Post-generation ===
Applying bone meal to nylium causes warped fungi to appear nearby, similar to flowers on grass blocks. While warped fungi and other Nether vegetation can generate this way, they are more likely to be the same type as the nylium.


== Usage ==


=== Placement ===

Warped fungi may be placed on grass blocks, dirt, coarse dirt, podzol, farmland, rooted dirt, moss blocks, nylium, mycelium, soul soil, mud and muddy mangrove roots. Attempting to place it on any other block fails. Warped fungi requires a solid block underneath it, and pops off and drops itself if it is no longer supported.
Warped fungi can also be placed in flower pots.


=== Growth ===
When bone meal is used on a warped fungus that is planted on matching nylium, it has a 40% chance to grow into its tall equivalent (similar to saplings and mushrooms). Warped fungi grow into huge warped fungi. The stem, shroomlight, or wart blocks may replace certain blocks including slabs and torches.


=== Breeding ===
Warped fungus can be used to breed striders and lead them around if crafted as a warped fungus on a stick.


=== Hoglins ===
All hoglins, regardless of age, avoid and run up to 7 blocks away from warped fungus when it is placed on a block or inside of a flower pot[Java Edition  only]. Held warped fungi and warped fungi on sticks do not have this effect.


=== Crafting ingredient ===


=== Composting ===
Placing a warped fungus into a composter has a 65% chance of raising the compost level by 1.
