Minecraft contains many music tracks, which play in various places in the game, including on the main menu, in Creative mode, in the Nether, in the End, and during the credits. The music tracks in the game are composed by C418, Lena Raine, Kumi Tanioka, Samuel Åberg, Aaron Cherof, Amos Roddy, and Hyper Potions.


== Playing ==
Music tracks can play at various points during gameplay. Some types of music are triggered by events, while others play randomly while certain criteria are met. The song "Alpha" always plays instantly during the credits, and the song "Boss" always plays instantly while the player is in the End if the ender dragon is undefeated or resummoned. Both of these tracks start playing in place of any already playing music. If the player is in the End and the ender dragon has been defeated, the normal End track plays 5 to 20 minutes after any currently playing tracks are finished.
If the player is underwater in an ocean or river biome, random underwater tracks play randomly 10 to 20 minutes after any currently playing tracks are finished.
If none of the previous criteria are met, but the player is in the Nether, a random general Nether track or a Nether biome-specific track plays, except warped forests, which don't have any music. If the player is not in the Nether and is currently in Creative mode, a random track plays (Creative only on Bedrock). If the player is not in Creative but is currently playing (i.e., not on the menu screen), a random general track plays. These tracks all play 10 to 20 minutes after any currently playing tracks are finished.
If a music track is playing while the player is entering a pale garden, it fades into silence. The music resumes when the player exits the biome.
If the player is on the menu screen, a random menu track starts playing after 1 to 30 seconds.
Menu music stops playing in Java Edition when the player leaves the main menu and stops playing in Bedrock Edition when a loading screen finishes. Game music stops playing when the player quits the world. When the game is paused, game music continues to play in Java Edition, while in Bedrock Edition it pauses and resumes when the player exits the pause menu.
Music also stops playing on lagging for a very long time, including under the highly complicated calculations like exploding a lot of TNT continuously, and when the memory usage is close to the allocation point and do not clear out for many seconds or even minutes.[verify]
All music as well as sounds in Minecraft are stored in the sound directory. In Java Edition, however, the sound files are obfuscated and cannot be directly accessed. To get around with this, the sound directory can be extracted by the methods showed in Tutorial:Sound directory. In Bedrock Edition, the music files are not obfuscated and can be found directly at game directory\data\resource_packs\vanilla_music\sounds\music.

In Java Edition, the player can change how frequently music plays by changing the "Music Frequency" setting in the "Music & Sounds" menu.
In Java Edition, when the "Show Music Toast" setting is enabled in the "Music & Sounds" menu, a toast showing the track name and artist appears on-screen when a new track starts playing. This toast is also shown in the pause menu.

== Legacy Console Edition ==
The Legacy Console Edition versions of the game had a lot of custom music created for its many Mash-up packs and mini games composed primarily by Gareth Coker, with some earlier Mash-up pack work and likely early Battle mini game work done by C418.


=== Mash-Up Packs ===


==== Festive Mash-Up Pack ====
This soundtrack was written by C418 and features tracks from Minecraft - Volume Beta and Christmas inspired arrangements of other tracks from Minecraft - Volume Beta. These special arrangements do not appear on an album and do not have names. The tracks for the battle map were written by Gareth Coker and appeared on the Minecraft: Battle & Tumble (Original Soundtrack) album. These files are found in DLC/Festive/Data.


==== Greek Mythology Mash-Up Pack ====
This original soundtrack was composed by Gareth Coker, with the exception of one track, and was inspired by ancient Greek literature and culture. The menu track for this pack is Seikilos' Epitaph, which is the oldest surviving complete musical composition in human history, understood to be written by a man named Seikilos. These files are found in DLC/Greek Mythology/Data.


==== Halloween 2015 Mash-Up Pack ====
This original soundtrack was written by C418 inspired by Halloween. Unlike other original Mash-Up Packs, none of these tracks have an official release nor names. The tracks for the battle map were written by Gareth Coker and appeared on the Minecraft: Battle & Tumble (Original Soundtrack) album. These files are found in DLC/Halloween 2015/Data.


==== Chinese Mythology Mash-Up Pack ====
This original soundtrack was composed by Gareth Coker and was inspired by Chinese literature and culture. Unlike most soundtracks written for the game, it features recurring motifs, in this case from the track "Xuanzang." These files are found in DLC/Chinese Mythology/Data.


==== Norse Mythology Mash-Up Pack ====
This original soundtrack was composed by Gareth Coker and was inspired by Norse literature and culture. These files are found in DLC/Norse Mythology/Data.


==== Egyptian Mythology Mash-Up Pack ====
This original soundtrack was composed by Gareth Coker and was inspired by Egyptian literature and culture. These files are found in DLC/Egyptian Mythology/Data.


==== Pirates of the Caribbean Mash-Up Pack ====
This original soundtrack was composed by Gareth Coker and was inspired by Disney's Pirates of the Caribbean franchise and its associated film soundtracks. Despite being a licensed mash-up pack, music from the source material was not used. This is the only complete original score written for Legacy Console Edition that has not been published as an album, and there are no titles for the tracks either. These files are found in DLC/Pirates of the Caribbean/Data.


=== Mini Games ===
The mini games for Legacy Console Edition also have their own unique soundtracks, composed by Gareth Coker and C418. Each map is assigned a set of music tracks, either by using a Mash-Up pack that supports custom music, or a hardcoded TrackList for packs that don't by using the map's ThemeID. These tracks can be found in the music folder, or audio folder for Xbox 360 Edition prior to TU57, at the root of the game files. Mini game tracks that are part of a Mash-up pack can be found inside of the Data folder inside of its respective DLC folder.


==== Battle ====
These music tracks play in the Battle mini game.


==== Tumble ====
These music tracks play in the Tumble mini game. While there were map themes made for special occasions and holidays, there were no unique tracks made for them. If a Mash-Up pack with custom music was used and it lacked Tumble music definition, it would default to using the Overworld music from that pack. Otherwise the default themes would play.


==== Glide ====
These music tracks play in the Glide mini game.


== Composers ==
Originally, all of Minecraft's music was composed by C418. According to Mojang, as the game got more popular (specifically starting with the Update Aquatic), others were hired to produce music and soundtracks for the game. However, C418 has cited legal disputes between himself and Microsoft, who wanted to own the music he produced outright, as the real reason for additional composers. Since the Nether Update, new tracks composed by Lena Raine, Kumi Tanioka, Samuel Åberg, Aaron Cherof, Amos Roddy and Hyper Potions were added. Samuel Åberg also composed many recent ambient sounds. Original music created for mash-up packs and Minigames, created by 4J Studios for Legacy Console Edition, were composed by Gareth Coker.

== Trivia ==
 
"The End" is heavily built on modified versions of several tracks from "Minecraft - Volume Alpha".
"calm3.ogg", or "Sweden", is a Passacaglia, with a changing melody over a repeated bass line.
On April Fools' Day 2014, Mojang played a bunch of villager themed pranks on the players, including temporarily replacing the track "hal1.ogg", or "Subwoofer Lullaby", with a version that had a villager singing along, as well as temporarily replacing all the menu songs with the villagers singing the Game Of Thrones theme song. This was reverted on April 2, 2014.
hal3.ogg (Haggstrom) is in septuple meter (precisely 7/4 time).
According to C418, the track "Mice on Venus" (game filename is piano3.ogg) was originally a short piano solo, but was then changed later in Minecraft's development and an addendum was added, which made the track what the game has currently.
The warped forest and pale garden are the only biomes in the game devoid of music, but they accomplish this in different ways.
The warped forest simply doesn't play music when the game would normally start playing a new track, but any music that started playing before entering a warped forest will continue playing. A sound event does exist for it (named music.nether.warped_forest in Java Edition and music.game.warped_forest in Bedrock Edition), but it is unused by default.
The pale garden has special behavior which fades out any currently-playing tracks upon entering the biome.
Despite never being implemented, the Legacy Console Edition code references the cancelled Build Off mini game with a call for MG04_01.binka. If the game's music player state is modified to be Build Off, the game will actually search for and play the file if it exists.
Overworld music does not play in Creative mode in Legacy Console Edition.
Some of the music tracks have a special title:
The word "Shuniji" (主虹, しゅにじ) means "primary rainbow" in Japanese.
The title of the track "So Below" is a reference to "As above, so below", a phrase associated with Hermeticism that is derived from a passage from the Emerald Tablet of Thoth, relating to the secrets of creating the philosopher's stone.
The word rubedo is Latin for "redness". The term was adopted by alchemists to refer to the final stage of creating the philosopher's stone.
The word chrysopoeia (χρυσοποιία) is an alchemical term that describes the creation of the philosopher's stone, or the transmutation of a given object into gold.
"Bromeliad" refers to a taxonomic family of flowering plants. This family contains pitcher plants and species resembling torchflowers.
