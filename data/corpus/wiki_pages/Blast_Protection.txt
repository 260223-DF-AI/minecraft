Blast Protection is an enchantment applied to armor that reduces damage taken from explosions and fireworks and increases explosion knockback resistance.


== Obtaining ==
Blast Protection can be obtained from enchanting tables, drops from mobs that have spawned in a raid[Bedrock Edition  only], books obtained by fishing, librarian villagers, and in the following structure loot chests: desert pyramids, strongholds, ocean ruins, ancient cities, woodland mansions, pillager outposts, mineshafts, monster rooms, jungle pyramids, and from vaults in trial chambers.


== Usage ==
Blast Protection reduces the damage the user takes from all explosions, including, but not limited to:

Fireballs shot by ghasts
Creepers/charged creepers
TNT
Beds/respawn anchors
End crystals
Wither and wither skulls
Firework rockets
Any damage type in the minecraft/tags/damage_type/is_explosion damage tag
The formula for damage reduction by explosions is (8  level)%, meaning that for every additional level another 8% of the damage is negated. The highest level (Blast Protection IV) absorbs 32% of the damage inflicted by any explosion.
In Java Edition, Blast Protection has the side effect of reducing knockback created from explosions by (15  level)%, stacking with multiple armor pieces. This also includes the knockback effect from wind charges and the Wind Burst enchantment.
Damage reduction from Protection, Fire Protection, Feather Falling, Blast Protection and Projectile Protection stacks up to an upper limit cap of 80% (see armor enchantments). Any further damage reduction that would normally be applied is instead ignored. This cap can be reached with 10 combined levels of Blast Protection on the player's armor, or by combining 12 levels of Protection with 4 levels of Blast Protection.


== Incompatibilities ==
Protection, Blast Protection, Fire Protection and Projectile Protection are mutually exclusive. Attempting to combine these enchantments with an anvil deletes the enchantment in the upgrade slot and replaces it with the enchantment in the sacrifice slot. However, if commands are used to force two or more of these enchantments on the same item, the effects stack. But when the total of the EPF provided by the protection enchantments surpasses 20, the rest of these enchantments will no longer directly reduce the damage.
