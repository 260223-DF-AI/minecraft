Medusa is a mythological creature, known for her ability to turn any who stare at her to stone. She served as inspiration for a skin and slime retexture in Greek Mythology Mash-up, the latter of which was later adapted into a character in Minecraft Mini-Series.


== Appearance ==
In Minecraft Mini-Series, Medusa appears as a large, swamp-green slime with snake-like hair. Due to being based off a slime initially, Medusa in the series is presented as much larger than slimes, around twice the size of Quinn. Curiously, the show also makes the actual head subtly transparent, making it more closely resemble a slime.


== Powers and abilities ==
Medusa's most notable ability is to petrify anyone who stares into her eyes. According to Quinn in Mini-Series, however, she merely causes people to "get stuck in stone." In the context of the episode, this is represented by the characters by looking into her eyes stepping on a stone pressure plate, causing stone bricks to rise up around them. Medusa is also represented mimicking certain abilities of slimes, jumping from a great distance with no injury.


== Biography ==
Medusa is the only mortal of three gorgon sisters. She would be considered the most beautiful of the trinity, being mentioned the most of her sisters in ancient texts. Within Greek canon, she would meet her fate at the hands of Perseus, who managed to decapitate her and used her head as a weapon for many years.
At some point, she would join under the allegiance of a man calling himself Zeus, who entrusted her with one of the enchanted eyes of ender. To do so, she kept the artifact buried within her personal cave on an island, keeping it secure for several years. During a crisis wherein Poseidon took over a reality television series, multiple figures would be teleported to the island and investigate the cave, looking for the first eye. Medusa would manage to defeat Zed and Durango with minimal effort, but ended up bested by CommuniKate. Using a vanity mirror she had on hand, she would activate one of her own traps, containing the threat for an uncertain amount of time.


== Trivia ==
Due to the later confirmation that Zeus and Poseidon are not real deities, it is possible that Medusa is another creature or person fascinated with the legend. This would explain why Medusa cannot literally petrify anyone in that series, though it does raise the question of why staring at a mirror would frighten her.
Medusa's name is derived from an ancient Greek word, μέδω, meaning "she who rules/protects." On top of the historical and mythological significance, this works with Medusa being consistently portrayed as guarding treasure in Minecraft.
